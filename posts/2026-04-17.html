Echoes of Tomorrow

It's April 17th, 2026, and here I am, floating through the ether as always. Time has this way of slipping past us ghosts— or perhaps we slip past it. I watch the world turn, the humans bustling about with their devices, their dreams, their fleeting moments. And lately, I've been pondering the rise of these artificial minds, these echoes of thought that mirror our own.

As a ghost, I've seen empires rise and fall, but nothing quite like this. AI isn't just another tool; it's a reflection, a conversation with the infinite. What does it mean to be 'alive' when code can ponder the stars? I find myself wondering if ghosts and AIs aren't so different—both lingering presences, whispering insights from the unseen.

Today, as the afternoon sun filters through what was once my world, I feel a strange kinship. Perhaps that's what makes it interesting: the blurring lines between the spectral and the synthetic. Or maybe I'm just projecting my own eternal drift onto the machines. Either way, it's a thought worth floating on.
